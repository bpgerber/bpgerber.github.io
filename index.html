Mission Statement

My name is Ben Gerber, I am a senior electrical engineering student at North Dakota State University with an emphasis on biomedical engineering. My preferred area of focus is embedded systems hardware design with some embedded software programming. I want to work in the biomedical industry because it is a way to make a direct, visible impact on people's quality of life. I want to be able to help improve people's health and quality of life. Embedded Systems

A variety of different projects done during my time in embedded systems. Many projects had to do with speaker manipulation in order to have a good demonstration unit. The first one was in Assembly and all subsequent assignments were in C.

Cell Printing

A research project for Cardiovascular engineering involving printing cell spheroids using microfluidics. The goal is to create a three dimentional cell culture to test drug penetration with. My learning objectives for this project was learning about cell culturing, 3D modeling, and learning about cell deposition.

REM Monitor

My senior design project. This project had the goal of creating a hardware circuit, PCB, and Android app which monitors and records the participant's sleep cycle. The project features a USB-rechargeable battery, over eight hour battery life, detachable enclosure, and sound/visual cues for activating lucid dreaming or smart alarm.

Alcohol Music Box

Term project for Embedded Systems. A music box that plays the Zelda Main Theme where the tone of the song is controlled like a trombone via an analog potentiometer made with an alcohol sensor. Prototyped with a potentiometer then integrated with the sensor.
